# 📖 Behavior School Curriculum & Learning Guide

Welcome to the **Behavior School Curriculum**. This document outlines the structured learning tracks, scientific foundations, and topic roadmaps across practical psychology, neuroscience, and human behavior.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By progressing through the Behavior School curriculum, learners will be able to:
1. **Identify Cognitive Traps**: Spot when cognitive biases (such as Confirmation Bias or the Zeigarnik Effect) are influencing personal choices.
2. **Master Behavior Change**: Implement friction engineering, habit loops, and identity shifts to build lasting habits without relying on brute-force willpower.
3. **Optimize Cognitive Performance**: Apply Active Recall, Spaced Repetition, and Deep Work protocols to learn complex skills 2x faster.
4. **Regulate Stress & Focus**: Understand dopamine baseline mechanics, cortisol stress responses, and sleep architecture to maintain high daily energy.
5. **Upgrade Decision Making**: Utilize mental models (First Principles, Second-Order Thinking, Inversion) to solve difficult problems systematically.

---

## 🏛️ Curriculum Tracks

### Track 1: Behavioral Change & Habit Architecture
* **Focus**: Actionable frameworks for changing daily behavior.
* **Core Concepts**:
  * The Cue → Craving → Response → Reward Loop
  * Friction Reduction vs Friction Engineering
  * Identity-Based Habits vs Outcome-Based Goals
  * Motivation vs Environmental Automation
* **Recommended Video**: *Why You Can't Stay Consistent*, *Why Motivation Always Fails*, *How Your Brain Creates Habits*.

---

### Track 2: Neuroscience & Cognitive Optimization
* **Focus**: Understanding the biological hardware of the human brain.
* **Core Concepts**:
  * **Dopamine Dynamics**: Baseline dopamine vs peak spikes; avoiding dopamine burnout.
  * **Neuroplasticity**: BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor), synaptic pruning, and structural rewiring.
  * **Memory Consolidation**: Hippocampal encoding, REM sleep memory sorting, and long-term potentiation.
  * **Attention Systems**: Prefrontal cortex executive function vs Default Mode Network (DMN) rumination.
* **Recommended Video**: *Dopamine Explained*, *Neuroplasticity Explained*, *Remember Anything Like a Pro*.

---

### Track 3: Accelerated Learning & Peak Focus
* **Focus**: Science-backed methods for acquiring skills rapidly.
* **Core Concepts**:
  * **Active Recall**: Testing retrieval over passive reading.
  * **Spaced Repetition**: Optimal review intervals (1, 3, 7, 14, 30 days).
  * **Feynman Technique**: Simplifying concepts to identify gaps in understanding.
  * **Deep Work & Flow State**: Triggers for entering 90-minute uninterrupted flow blocks.
* **Recommended Video**: *How to Learn Faster*, *Learn Any Skill as Fast as Possible*.

---

### Track 4: Psychology & Mental Models
* **Focus**: Frameworks that improve critical thinking and reveal hidden biases.
* **Core Concepts**:
  * **Cognitive Dissonance**: Rationalization loops and belief protection.
  * **First Principles Thinking**: Deconstructing problems down to fundamental physical truths.
  * **Second-Order Thinking**: Asking "And then what?" to anticipate long-term consequences.
  * **Inversion**: Solving problems by working backwards from avoiding failure.
* **Recommended Video**: *Why Smart People Still Procrastinate*, *Stop Overthinking Everything*, *Cognitive Biases Explained*.

---

## 📌 Recommended Beginner Learning Path

1. **Step 1**: Watch *You Are Not Lazy* to reframe personal action vs inaction through neuroscience.
2. **Step 2**: Complete the interactive **"Why Do I Do This?" Mind Diagnostic** on [behavior-school.github.io](https://behavior-school.github.io/#diagnostic).
3. **Step 3**: Study the **6 Core Content Pillars** and select 1 behavioral habit to optimize this week.
4. **Step 4**: Review the **Mental Models & Cognitive Biases Library** to identify personal decision-making biases.
